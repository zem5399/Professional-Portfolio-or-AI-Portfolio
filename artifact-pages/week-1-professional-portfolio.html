Week 1 Artifact

AI and Machine Learning History Timeline

A professional portfolio artifact presenting major milestones in the development of artificial intelligence and machine learning, from early theoretical foundations to modern generative and multimodal AI systems.

AI History Professional Portfolio Research and Communication Audience-Focused Design

Introduction

Professional portfolios are used across many fields to demonstrate experience, education, professional growth, and practical skills. In the rapidly changing artificial intelligence and machine learning field, a portfolio allows students and professionals to show more than theoretical knowledge. It provides evidence of technical understanding, problem-solving ability, communication skills, and continued professional development.

Description

This assignment required me to select a free portfolio tool and add my first audience-specific artifact using work completed during Workshop 1. The available options included an AI and machine learning timeline, AI lab work, or a professional assessment.

I selected the AI and Machine Learning History Timeline because it demonstrates my understanding of how the field developed from early theoretical ideas to modern generative AI technologies.

Objective

The objective was to create a professional portfolio artifact for a specific audience, communicate my unique professional value, and present technical information in a way that is understandable to both technical and non-technical readers.

Target Audience and Purpose

This artifact was designed for potential employers, professional partners, and others evaluating my preparedness in artificial intelligence and machine learning.

I kept each milestone brief and avoided unnecessary technical language so readers with different levels of technical knowledge could understand the significance of each event. The timeline presents the subject as a progression from the early foundations of AI, through the deep-learning revolution, to the current period of generative and multimodal AI.

Selected Milestones

Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

1950

Turing Test Proposed

Alan Turing raised the question of whether machines could think, helping establish the philosophical foundation for artificial intelligence.

1956

Dartmouth Conference

A summer workshop at Dartmouth College formally established artificial intelligence as an academic field of study.

1997

Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov, becoming an important public milestone in AI.

2012

AlexNet Breakthrough

A deep convolutional neural network dramatically improved image-recognition performance and helped launch the modern deep-learning era.

2016

AlphaGo Defeats Lee Sedol

DeepMind's AlphaGo defeated a leading Go champion in a game previously considered too complex for computers to master.

2020

GPT-3 Released

GPT-3 demonstrated that large language models could generate highly fluent and human-like text across many tasks.

2022

ChatGPT Launches

ChatGPT introduced generative AI to a broad public audience and changed how many people interact with AI systems.

2023–2024

Multimodal AI Expansion

Models capable of processing combinations of text, images, audio, and video became increasingly available and widely adopted.

Process

I first reviewed the portfolio requirements and considered the intended audience for the artifact. I then selected the AI and machine learning timeline because it provided a clear way to demonstrate my understanding of the field's development.

I organized the milestones into a chronological sequence and focused on events that were significant to the evolution of artificial intelligence. I also considered which details to include and which to leave out so the timeline would remain focused, readable, and relevant to the intended audience.

Skills Demonstrated

  • Professional portfolio development
  • Audience-focused communication
  • Research and information organization
  • Technical concepts explained in accessible language
  • Historical analysis of artificial intelligence
  • Self-assessment and professional reflection

Reflection

This artifact helped me think more intentionally about what an outside audience needs from a professional portfolio item. It was easy to collect dates and facts, but more challenging to decide what information should be included so the timeline remained focused and did not become a complete history of AI.

The assignment also showed me the importance of selecting a portfolio platform that can be updated as technology and my professional experience continue to change. I plan to evaluate future artifacts by asking how they demonstrate my professional skills and what information my intended audience needs.
