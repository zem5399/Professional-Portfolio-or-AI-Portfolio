Introduction

This artifact presents a practical prompt engineering exercise in which I created, tested, evaluated, and improved prompts for a generative AI tool.

Together with my AI history timeline and professional skills self-assessment, this artifact shows how my knowledge and self-awareness can be applied to a real artificial intelligence task.

Description

The exercise documents the original prompt, the AI-generated response, the weaknesses identified in that response, and the revised instructions used to improve the final output.

I selected this artifact because it demonstrates practical prompt engineering rather than simply describing my skills. It gives readers an opportunity to see how I analyze an AI response and improve it through structured revisions.

Objective

The objective was to use a generative AI tool to complete a defined task, evaluate the quality of the output, identify unclear or missing instructions, and revise the prompt until the result consistently matched the task requirements.

Target Audience and Purpose

This artifact is intended for prospective employers, technical team leaders, instructors, and professional partners who want to see practical evidence of my ability to work with generative AI tools.

Instead of presenting only the completed output, the artifact preserves the earlier prompts and revisions so the audience can evaluate my reasoning and improvement process.

Iterative Workflow

Prompt Engineering Cycle

01

Draft Prompt

Begin with an initial instruction that describes the task and desired result.

02

Generate Output

Submit the prompt to the generative AI system and review the response it produces.

03

Evaluate Against the Task

Compare the output with the original requirements and identify missing, unclear, biased, or inaccurate elements.

04

Revise Prompt

Add clearer requirements, relevant context, constraints, and examples before testing the prompt again.

Process

I began with a simple prompt and reviewed the response produced by the generative AI tool. I then identified areas where the instructions were vague or where the output did not fully match the task.

I rewrote the prompt to include more detailed requirements, additional background information, and relevant examples. This process was repeated until the output consistently met the intended purpose.

The most challenging part was resisting the temptation to accept the first workable response. The real learning came from identifying why an output was not good enough and determining which instruction needed improvement.

Responsible AI Considerations

The generated responses were not accepted automatically. They were reviewed for accuracy, validity, relevance, and possible bias.

This reflects the importance of using AI as a support tool while maintaining human judgment, accountability, and professional responsibility.

Reflection

This artifact helped me understand prompt writing as an iterative process rather than a skill based only on intuition. Creating a prompt and accepting a mediocre response is easy; identifying the missing instruction and improving the output requires deeper analysis.

The exercise also reinforced the importance of evidence. Just as my self-assessment required examples to support each rating, this artifact demonstrates effectiveness through the visible progression from an initial prompt to an improved result.

In future AI work, I will continue asking not only what I accomplished, but also what evidence another person could use to judge whether the result was effective.
